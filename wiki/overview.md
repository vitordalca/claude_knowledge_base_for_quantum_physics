## Current State (Post-Ingest #3)

The wiki now contains foundational knowledge about **antimatter production, storage, and fundamental symmetries**, based on Veritasium's comprehensive documentary on CERN's antimatter factory.

### Core Knowledge Areas

**1. Antimatter Physics**
The wiki establishes that [[concepts/antimatter]] is a fundamental class of particles predicted by Dirac's relativistic equation (1928) and produced daily at CERN. Antiparticles are mirror opposites of matter: same mass, opposite charge. When matter and antimatter collide, they annihilate into pure energy (E=mc²).

**2. The Matter-Antimatter Mystery**
Central to the knowledge base is [[concepts/matter-antimatter-asymmetry]] — perhaps the biggest unsolved problem in physics. The early universe created matter and antimatter equally; yet 99.9999999% annihilated, leaving only one billionth of matter surviving to form all stars and life. No agreed-upon explanation exists; the Standard Model predicts only 10⁻¹⁸ of the observed asymmetry, requiring new physics.

**3. Experimental Capability**
CERN's [[entities/cern]] antimatter factory produces ~20 million [[concepts/antiproton]] antiprotons per minute by colliding high-energy protons with iridium targets. [[concepts/penning-trap]] superconducting electromagnetic traps, operated at 4 Kelvin in ultra-high vacuum, enable storage of antiprotons for 600+ days—a transformative breakthrough.

**4. Fundamental Symmetry Tests**
[[concepts/cpt-symmetry]] is the cornerstone of quantum field theory and special relativity. Tests at CERN (particularly the BASE experiment) show that antiprotons and protons are equivalent under CPT transformation to parts per 10 billion, validating Einstein's framework while leaving open the mystery of how CPT-symmetric laws can produce matter-antimatter asymmetry.

**5. Historical Context**
The wiki captures pivotal moments: [[entities/paul-dirac]]'s 1928 prediction of antiparticles, [[entities/chien-shiung-wu]]'s 1957 parity violation experiment (excluded from Nobel Prize despite leading the experiment), and subsequent discoveries of CP violation. Each revealed that physics is less symmetric than believed, yet each failed to explain matter-antimatter asymmetry.

### Key Experiments

Three cutting-edge CERN experiments drive current antimatter research:
- **ALPHA-g** — Measuring gravitational acceleration of antihydrogen atoms; preliminary result: antimatter falls down (no antigravity), but with large measurement uncertainty
- **BASE** — Testing CPT symmetry via antiproton magnetic moment measurements
- **GBAR** — Developing ultra-cold antihydrogen ions for 1% precision gravity measurement (in progress)

### Knowledge Gaps and Open Questions

The wiki has identified several frontiers:
1. **Baryogenesis Mechanism** — What breaks CP symmetry asymmetrically in the early universe?
2. **Gravity and Antimatter** — Does gravity treat antimatter identically to matter? ALPHA-g's large error bars leave this open.
3. **Antimatter Distribution** — Can antiprotons be transported and studied at institutions worldwide? (Portable trap developed March 2026)
4. **Leptogenesis vs. Electroweak Baryogenesis** — Which mechanism(s) generate lepton/baryon asymmetry?

**Layer 3: Experimental Validation (Antimatter Physics)**
- [[sources/how-we-make-antimatter.md]] shows CERN produces/stores antiparticles predicted by QFT

**The Integrated Picture**: 
- **QFT** (Layer 1) defines what particles are
- **Higgs mechanism** (Layer 2) explains *why* they have mass (exception: photon, gluon)
- **Experiment** (Layer 3) confirms both frameworks and explores open mysteries

QFT predicts antimatter exists. CERN confirms and studies it. QFT + Higgs predicts particle masses and couplings. CERN measures them. But one great mystery remains unsolved: **Why is there more matter than antimatter?** The fact that antimatter exists at all is explained by QFT; the 10⁹:1 asymmetry is not, and this drives current research in leptogenesis and electroweak baryogenesis.
