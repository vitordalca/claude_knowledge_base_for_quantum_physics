## Summary

The Big Bang is the physical theory that the universe expanded from an initial state of extremely high density and temperature approximately 13.8 billion years ago. At the moment of the Big Bang, all matter, energy, and spacetime itself originated from a singular point (the singularity). As the universe expanded and cooled, it underwent phase transitions: first a quark-gluon plasma, then free quarks and electrons, then bound protons and neutrons, then light nuclei, and finally neutral atoms. The Big Bang theory is supported by multiple independent observations: the cosmic microwave background (relic radiation from ~380,000 years after the Big Bang), the abundance of light elements, and the expansion of galaxies. Within the first seconds, the conditions were so extreme that particle physics (including antimatter creation) played a dominant role.

## Resumo

O Big Bang é a teoria física de que o universo expandiu de um estado inicial de densidade e temperatura extremamente altas aproximadamente 13,8 bilhões de anos atrás. No momento do Big Bang, toda matéria, energia e espaço-tempo em si originaram de um ponto singular (a singularidade). Conforme o universo se expandiu e resfriou, passou por transições de fase: primeiro um plasma de quarks-glúons, depois quarks e elétrons livres, depois prótons e nêutrons ligados, depois leves núcleos, e finalmente átomos neutros. A teoria do Big Bang é apoiada por múltiplas observações independentes: a radiação cósmica de fundo em micro-ondas (radiação relíquia de ~380.000 anos após o Big Bang), a abundância de elementos leves e a expansão das galáxias. Nos primeiros segundos, as condições eram tão extremas que a física de partículas (incluindo criação de antimatéria) desempenhou um papel dominante.

## Explanation

### The Cosmic Timeline

The Big Bang timeline describes the universe's evolution from an infinitesimal size to today:

| Time After Big Bang | Temperature | Event |
|---|---|---|
| 0 s | ∞ | Singularity; all physics breaks down |
| 10⁻⁴³ s (Planck time) | 10³² K | Quantum gravity needed; beyond current theory |
| 10⁻³⁶ to 10⁻³² s | 10²⁷ K | Cosmic inflation (exponential expansion) |
| 10⁻⁶ s | 10¹³ K | Quark-gluon plasma → free quarks, electrons, photons |
| 10⁻³ s | 10¹¹ K | Quarks combine into protons, neutrons, nuclei |
| 3 s | 10¹⁰ K | Photons too weak for pair production; matter-antimatter annihilation complete |
| 380,000 yr | 3000 K | Electrons bind to nuclei; atoms form; photons decouple (CMB released) |
| 100–200 Myr | 30 K | First stars and galaxies form |
| 13.8 Gyr | 2.7 K | Today; universe expanding, cooling, dominated by dark energy |

### Early Universe Particle Physics

In the first fractions of a second, the universe was a cauldron of particle creation and annihilation:

**Pair Creation and Annihilation (first second)**:
- Photons with energy > mc² (where m is particle mass) constantly created particle-antiparticle pairs
- Each pair immediately annihilated, releasing photons
- This equilibrium: γ ↔ particle + antiparticle occurred repeatedly

**The Crisis**: If matter and antimatter were created in equal amounts, they should have annihilated completely, leaving only radiation. Yet we observe a universe dominated by matter. This **matter-antimatter asymmetry** is one of physics' deepest mysteries (see [[concepts/matter-antimatter-asymmetry]]).

### Evidence for the Big Bang

**Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB)**: Discovered accidentally in 1964, the CMB is relic radiation from ~380,000 years after the Big Bang. It fills all of space with a temperature of ~2.7 K and exhibits tiny temperature fluctuations (parts per 100,000) that match predictions. Planck satellite measurements (2018) pin the universe's age at 13.787 ± 0.02 billion years.

**Abundance of Light Elements**: The early universe was hot enough to synthesize deuterium, ³He, ⁴He, and trace ⁷Li. Big Bang nucleosynthesis predictions match observed abundances in old stars and gas clouds to within percent level.

**Hubble Expansion**: Edwin Hubble's 1929 observation that galaxies recede from us with velocity proportional to distance (v = H₀d) provides direct evidence that the universe is expanding. Extrapolating backward gives a birth time of ~13.8 Gyr.

**Large-scale Structure**: Computer simulations of gravitational collapse under the Big Bang framework predict galaxy distributions that match observations, confirming the underlying cosmology.

### Inflationary Cosmology

**Cosmic Inflation** (Guth, Linde, 1980s) proposes that the universe underwent exponential expansion in the first 10⁻³⁶ to 10⁻³² seconds. This solves several puzzles:

- **Horizon problem**: The CMB is uniform across regions that should have been causally disconnected. Inflation solves this by expanding a single causally-connected region.
- **Flatness problem**: The universe appears geometrically flat (Ω_total ≈ 1). Inflation naturally produces a flat universe.
- **Smoothness problem**: Why is the universe so homogeneous on large scales? Inflation explains this.

Inflation's predictions have been confirmed by CMB observations, earning Alan Guth and Andrei Linde recognition as architects of modern cosmology.

## Current State of the Field

### Open Questions

1. **What happened at t=0?** Classical general relativity predicts a singularity where density → ∞ and physics breaks down. Quantum gravity must describe the first 10⁻⁴³ seconds.

2. **Why is there more matter than antimatter?** CP-violation in the Standard Model cannot explain the observed asymmetry by a factor of >10¹⁸. New physics is required.

3. **What is dark matter?** ~85% of matter is invisible; its nature remains unknown. Candidates include WIMPs, axions, and sterile neutrinos.

4. **What is dark energy?** ~68% of the universe's energy density is dark energy, driving accelerated expansion. Its nature is profoundly mysterious.

5. **Did inflation actually occur?** Strong evidence supports inflation, but the inflaton field (presumed particle causing inflation) has never been detected directly.

### Active Research Frontiers

- **LIGO gravitational wave detectors**: Searching for primordial gravitational waves from the Big Bang
- **Large-scale surveys**: Mapping billions of galaxies to test cosmological models
- **Precision CMB measurements**: Next-generation experiments seeking deviations from standard cosmology
- **Antimatter searches**: CERN and others seek evidence of matter-antimatter asymmetry mechanisms

## Open Questions

- **Can quantum gravity explain the singularity?** A consistent quantum theory of spacetime could resolve infinities
- **Is the Big Bang truly the beginning?** Some cyclic universe models propose infinite cycles; observational tests are advancing
- **Are there other universes?** Inflationary multiverse concepts are speculative but testable in principle
- **When will the universe end?** Current observations suggest eternal expansion (Big Freeze); other endpoints (Big Crunch, Big Rip) depend on future evolution
