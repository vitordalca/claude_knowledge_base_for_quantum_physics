## Summary

CPT symmetry is the combined symmetry of **charge conjugation (C)**, **parity reflection (P)**, and **time reversal (T)**. It states that the laws of physics are invariant under simultaneous reversal of all three operations: swap all charges with opposite charges, reflect space (mirror the universe), and reverse time. CPT symmetry is built into the structure of special relativity and quantum field theory; it is a fundamental pillar of modern physics. Breaking CPT would invalidate both the Standard Model and special relativity, making CPT symmetry perhaps the most sacred symmetry in physics.

## Resumo

A simetria CPT é a simetria combinada de **conjugação de carga (C)**, **reflexão de paridade (P)** e **reversão temporal (T)**. Ela afirma que as leis da física são invariantes sob inversão simultânea dessas três operações: trocar todas as cargas por cargas opostas, refletir o espaço (espelhar o universo) e reverter o tempo. A simetria CPT está embutida na estrutura da relatividade especial e da teoria quântica de campos; é um pilar fundamental da física moderna. Quebrar CPT invalidaria tanto o Modelo Padrão quanto a relatividade especial, tornando a simetria CPT talvez a simetria mais sagrada da física.

## Explanation

### The Three Symmetries

**Charge Conjugation (C)**
- Swap all particles with their antiparticles
- Positive charges become negative, and vice versa
- The statement: "Physics should work the same if all positive charges were negative and all negative charges were positive"
- Intuition: There is nothing special about the sign of a charge; it's arbitrary

**Parity Reflection (P)**
- Mirror the universe: (x, y, z) → (−x, −y, −z)
- All left-handed objects become right-handed and vice versa
- The statement: "Physics should work the same in our universe and its mirror image"
- Intuition: The laws of physics shouldn't distinguish left from right

**Time Reversal (T)**
- Run all processes backward in time: t → −t
- Particles' velocities reverse, but charges and spins remain the same
- The statement: "Physics should work the same whether time runs forward or backward"
- Caveat: This seems violated (eggs unscramble, ice melts). But this violation comes from the **second law of thermodynamics** (statistical law), not fundamental physics. At the particle level, individual collisions are time-reversible.

### Individual Violations (1956–1965)

For decades, physicists believed C, P, and T were individually conserved. Then came shocks:

**Parity Violation (1957)** — [[concepts/parity-violation]]
- Chien-Shiung Wu's experiment showed the weak force violates parity
- Cobalt-60 decay prefers left-handed emission of electrons
- Shattered the belief in mirror symmetry

**Charge Violation** — Shortly after, some processes were found to violate charge symmetry too.

**CP Violation (1964)**
- Observed in kaon decay
- Matter and antimatter behave slightly differently in weak interactions
- But insufficient to explain matter-antimatter asymmetry

### CPT Symmetry as the Last Bastion

As individual symmetries crumbled, the question became: **Is CPT inviolable?**

The answer is essentially "yes" at a deep mathematical level. Here's why:

**Theorem (Schwinger, Lüders, Pauli; 1951–1954):** If the laws of physics obey special relativity (Lorentz invariance), then they must automatically obey CPT symmetry. The proof is quite elegant:

1. Lorentz transformations relate observers in different reference frames
2. Relativity requires all inertial observers to measure identical speed of light
3. Quantum field theory built on this Lorentz structure
4. The structure mathematically *requires* CPT invariance

**Converse:** If CPT symmetry breaks, then Lorentz invariance breaks, and special relativity collapses.

### CPT Implications

**Particle-Antiparticle Symmetry**
- If a particle has mass M, charge Q, and magnetic moment μ, its antiparticle must have mass M, charge −Q, and magnetic moment −μ
- No exceptions allowed (except photon/Higgs, which are self-conjugate)

**Energy Relationships**
- A process A → B + C in forward time must be microscopically equivalent (via CPT) to the antiparticle process Ā → B̄ + C̄ in reverse time

**Reaction Rates**
- Cross-section for process A → B must equal cross-section for Ā → B̄ under CPT

## Current State of the Field

### Testing CPT Symmetry

Physicists test CPT by comparing matter and antimatter properties to unprecedented precision:

| Measurement | Particle | Antiparticle | Precision | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mass (proton/antiproton) | — | — | 1 part in 10¹⁰ | Consistent |
| Magnetic Moment | Antiproton | Proton | 1 part in 10⁹ | Consistent (BASE exp.) |
| Charge-to-Mass Ratio | Antiproton | Proton | 1 part in 10¹⁰ | Consistent |
| Gravitational Acceleration | Antihydrogen | Hydrogen | ~25% (poor precision) | Consistent (ALPHA-g) |

**Key Experiment: BASE (CERN)**
- Measures antiproton magnetic moment using a Penning trap
- Result: (−2.792847344 ± 0.000000084) × μN
- Proton magnetic moment: (2.792847356 ± 0.000000023) × μN
- Difference: **Fully consistent with CPT symmetry** to better than 1 part in 10 billion

### CPT Tests Beyond Antimatter

- **Neutral Kaons** — Measure CP violation while maintaining CPT; stringent tests of CPT in weak sector
- **Fundamental Constants** — Measure fine-structure constant and electron magnetic moment; look for variation with time or position (would violate CPT)
- **Gravitational Redshift Tests** — Compare matter and antimatter in Earth's gravitational field; GBAR experiment upcoming

### Why CPT Is Special

CPT is unique among symmetries because:
1. It's mandatory, not optional (built into relativistic quantum field theory)
2. Breaking it would invalidate special relativity itself
3. It connects matter and antimatter at a fundamental level
4. No deviations have ever been observed, despite probing to exquisite precision

## Open Questions

- **Will future precision tests find a CPT violation?** Current limits are parts per 10 billion; can we reach parts per trillion?
- **If CPT is broken, what new physics would emerge?** Would require modification of special relativity and quantum field theory.
- **Does CPT symmetry hold in gravity?** Unlike electromagnetic and weak forces, gravity doesn't couple naturally to quantum field theory; CPT status in quantum gravity unclear.
- **Could CPT violation explain matter-antimatter asymmetry?** Theoretically possible but would require radical revision of fundamental physics.
