## Summary

The Higgs field is a scalar quantum field that permeates all of space and is the only known scalar (spin-0) field among the fundamental fields of the Standard Model. The Higgs field has a non-zero vacuum expectation value (vev ≈ 246 GeV), meaning that even in its lowest-energy state, the field fills space with a constant, non-zero background value. Through the Higgs mechanism, this field couples to other particles and generates their masses. Without the Higgs field, all fundamental particles would be massless. The Higgs boson is the quantum excitation (particle) of the Higgs field, discovered in 2012 at CERN's LHC with mass of approximately 125 GeV/c².

## Resumo

O campo de Higgs é um campo quântico escalar que permeia todo o espaço e é o único campo escalar (spin-0) conhecido entre os campos fundamentais do Modelo Padrão. O campo de Higgs tem um valor de expectativa de vácuo não-nulo (vev ≈ 246 GeV), significando que até em seu estado de menor energia, o campo preenche o espaço com um valor de fundo constante e não-nulo. Através do mecanismo de Higgs, este campo se acopla com outras partículas e gera suas massas. Sem o campo de Higgs, todas as partículas fundamentais seriam sem massa. O bóson de Higgs é a excitação quântica (partícula) do campo de Higgs, descoberto em 2012 no LHC do CERN com massa de aproximadamente 125 GeV/c².

## Explanation

### Field Nature and Uniqueness

The Higgs field is unique among the four fundamental forces and their carrier fields:

| Field | Spin | Type | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Electromagnetic | 1 | Vector | Carries EM force; massless photons |
| Weak (W, Z) | 1 | Vector | Carries weak force; massive bosons |
| Strong (gluon) | 1 | Vector | Carries strong force; massless gluons |
| **Higgs** | **0** | **Scalar** | **Generates mass via coupling** |

Scalars are the simplest fields mathematically—they have no directional dependence, just a single value at each point in space. Fermions (electrons, quarks) have spin-1/2; vector bosons (photons, W/Z) have spin-1. The Higgs is fundamentally different.

### Vacuum Expectation Value (VEV)

In quantum field theory, the ground state (vacuum) of a field is not empty—fields have quantum fluctuations. For most fields, the VEV is zero (the field oscillates around zero). For the Higgs field, something extraordinary occurs:

**In today's cool universe (T << 159.5 GeV/kB)**:
- The Higgs field's potential energy is minimized at a **non-zero value**
- This non-zero background value is the VEV: **v ≈ 246 GeV**
- The field sits in a potential well away from the origin (see [[concepts/mexican-hat-potential]])

**Consequence**: The entire universe is bathed in a non-zero Higgs field. Every particle that interacts with this field acquires mass proportional to its coupling strength:
**m = g·v/c²**
where g is the coupling constant and v = 246 GeV.

### The Higgs Mechanism

The Higgs mechanism is the process by which mass emerges:

1. **Electroweak Symmetry**: At high temperatures (early universe), the weak and electromagnetic forces are unified and indistinguishable. All particles are massless.

2. **Symmetry Breaking**: As the universe cools below ~159.5 GeV, the Higgs field develops a non-zero VEV. This is **spontaneous symmetry breaking**—the field "chooses" one of many equivalent ground states.

3. **Mass Acquisition**: Once the Higgs field acquires its VEV:
   - W and Z bosons gain mass (m_W ≈ 80 GeV, m_Z ≈ 91 GeV)
   - Fermions (electrons, quarks, muons) couple to the Higgs field and gain mass
   - Photons remain massless (no coupling to Higgs)

4. **Higgs Boson**: The quantum oscillation around the VEV is the Higgs boson, with mass m_H ≈ 125 GeV.

### Coupling Hierarchies

Different particles couple to the Higgs field with different strengths:

**Strong coupling** (high mass):
- Top quark: m_t ≈ 173 GeV (couples most strongly)
- W and Z bosons: m ≈ 80–91 GeV
- Bottom quark: m_b ≈ 4.2 GeV

**Weak coupling** (low mass):
- Muon: m_μ ≈ 0.106 GeV
- Electron: m_e ≈ 0.000511 GeV
- Up and down quarks: m_u, m_d ≈ 0.003–0.006 GeV

**No coupling**:
- Photons (massless)
- Gluons (massless)
- Neutrinos (massless in Standard Model, though experiments suggest tiny masses)

## Current State of the Field

### Higgs Boson Measurements (Post-2012 Discovery)

Since the 2012 discovery, precision measurements have confirmed:
- **Mass**: 125.10 ± 0.14 GeV
- **Spin**: Consistent with spin-0 (scalar)
- **CP symmetry**: Appears CP-even (symmetric under parity-charge reversal)
- **Coupling strengths**: Measured to match Standard Model predictions to ~10% precision
- **Decay modes**: Higgs → γγ, ZZ, WW, bb confirmed; rare decays observed

### Open Questions

1. **Is the Higgs field fundamental?** Some theories propose the Higgs emerges from composite structures at higher energies

2. **Why is the Higgs mass ~125 GeV?** The origin of this value has no explanation in the Standard Model—it is an empirical input

3. **Vacuum stability**: Calculations suggest the current Higgs mass places the electroweak vacuum in a metastable state—potentially unstable over cosmic timescales. Could the vacuum tunnel to a lower energy state? Unlikely to occur within the age of the universe.

4. **Multiple Higgs fields?** Some beyond-Standard-Model theories propose additional scalar fields (e.g., supersymmetry predicts 5 Higgs bosons)
