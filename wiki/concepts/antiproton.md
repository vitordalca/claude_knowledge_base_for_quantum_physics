## Summary

An antiproton is the antimatter counterpart of the proton: it has the same mass (938 MeV/c²), the same spin (1/2), but opposite charge (−1 elementary charge, versus +1 for the proton). Antiprotons are produced in high-energy collisions between fast protons and target nuclei; CERN's antimatter factory produces ~20 million antiprotons per minute. Antiprotons are confined in Penning traps for months to years, enabling precision tests of matter-antimatter symmetry and serving as the building block for antihydrogen atoms used in gravity experiments.

## Resumo

Um antiproton é o equivalente de antimatéria do próton: tem a mesma massa (938 MeV/c²), o mesmo spin (1/2), mas carga oposta (−1 carga elementar, versus +1 para o próton). Antiprotons são produzidos em colisões de alta energia entre prótons rápidos e núcleos alvo; a fábrica de antimatéria do CERN produz ~20 milhões de antiprotons por minuto. Antiprotons são confinados em armadilhas de Penning por meses a anos, permitindo testes de precisão da simetria matéria-antimatéria e servindo como bloco de construção para átomos de anti-hidrogênio usados em experimentos de gravidade.

## Explanation

### Particle Properties

**Mass and Composition**
- Like protons, antiprotons are **baryons**—composite particles made of three quarks (or antiquarks)
- A proton is composed of 2 up quarks + 1 down quark (uud)
- An antiproton is composed of 2 anti-up quarks + 1 anti-down quark (ūū d̄)
- Mass: 938.27 MeV/c² (essentially identical to proton mass)
- Mean lifetime (free): Extremely long when isolated; only annihilates on contact with matter

**Charge**
- Electric charge: −1 (one negative charge unit)
- Proton charge: +1
- This opposite charge enables confinement in electric fields

**Magnetic Moment**
- Antiproton magnetic moment: −2.792847... μN (nuclear magnetons)
- Proton magnetic moment: +2.792847... μN
- Ratio: −1.0000 (within 1 part in 10⁹), consistent with CPT symmetry

**Spin**
- Intrinsic angular momentum: ℏ/2 (same as proton)
- Quantum spin state: 1/2 (fermion)

### Production Mechanism

**Target Collision**
- High-energy protons (99.93% light speed, ~26 GeV) from CERN's Proton Synchrotron
- Collide with dense iridium target (3 mm diameter, 55 mm long)
- Iridium chosen for high atomic number (77) → many nuclei packed densely

**Quark-Antiquark Pair Creation**
When a fast proton penetrates an iridium nucleus and collides with a neutron or proton:
1. The collision releases enormous energy
2. Strong force inside the nucleon breaks quark bonds
3. Surplus energy converts into **quark-antiquark pairs** (Bremsstrahlung-like process)
4. Multiple pairs created in shower: uū, d d̄, s s̄, c c̄, etc.
5. Occasionally, 2 anti-up + 1 anti-down combine into an antiproton
6. Complementary reaction: 2 up + 1 down → new proton

**Efficiency**
- Production rate: ~20 million antiprotons per minute
- Percentage of collisions yielding antiprotons: ~0.01%
- Energy needed: Each antiproton costs ~26 GeV to produce (compared to intrinsic mass of 938 MeV)

### Historical Discovery

**Prediction** — Paul Dirac (1928): Relativistic quantum equation predicts antielectrons; by analogy, antiprotons should exist

**First Observation** — Emilio Segrè and Owen Chamberlain (1955): Found antiprotons in debris of proton-nucleus collisions using Berkeley's Bevatron accelerator. Nobel Prize (1959).

**CERN Era** — From 1980s onward: CERN's Proton Synchrotron dedicated to antiproton production; millions per minute now routine.

## Current State of the Field

### Storage and Confinement (CERN)

**Penning Trap Technology**
- Antiprotons decelerated from 96% to 10% light speed in the **Antiproton Decelerator**
- Further slowed to 1.5% light speed via **ELENA** (Extra Low Energy Antiproton ring)
- Trapped in **superconducting Penning traps** at 4 Kelvin, ultra-high vacuum
- Current storage record: **614 days** (BASE experiment, 2020)

**Accumulation**
- Antiprotons collected and cooled over minutes to hours
- Clouds of 10⁸–10⁹ antiprotons accumulated for experiments
- Efficiency improved from 0.1% to 86% via decades of optimization

### Precision Measurements

**BASE Experiment (CERN)**
- Measures antiproton magnetic moment
- Precision: 1 part in 10⁹
- Result: Consistent with CPT symmetry
- Validates proton-antiproton equivalence

**Future Portable Distribution**
- March 2026: CERN loaded 92 antiprotons into portable trap, transported on truck
- Vision: Distribute antiprotons to other institutions worldwide
- Enables antimatter research without requiring proximity to production factory

### Use in Antihydrogen Production

**GBAR Experiment**
1. Accumulate positrons in trap
2. Produce positronium (electron-positron pair)
3. Beam antiprotons through positronium cloud
4. Antiproton captures positron → antihydrogen atom (neutral)
5. Antihydrogen escapes magnetic trap, falls under gravity
6. Measure gravitational acceleration to test CPT and gravity

**ALPHA-g Experiment**
1. Merge antiproton cloud with positron cloud in Penning trap
2. Some antiprotons capture positrons → antihydrogen
3. Use magnetic trap to capture neutral antihydrogen atoms
4. Carefully weaken trap; measure release rates through top vs. bottom
5. Infer whether antihydrogen falls up (antigravity) or down

Result (2023): Antihydrogen falls down, ~75% of normal gravity (large error bars).

## Open Questions

- **How long can antiprotons be stored?** Current record 614 days; fundamental limit unknown.
- **Can antiprotons be cooled further?** Laser cooling and sympathetic cooling with ions in progress.
- **Will portable traps enable antimatter science at multiple institutions?** First distribution occurred March 2026; scaling is next frontier.
- **Can antiprotons shed light on matter-antimatter asymmetry?** Precision measurements may constrain new physics models.
- **Are there antimatter sources in nature we haven't discovered?** Cosmic rays, solar wind, etc.; mostly matter dominates.
