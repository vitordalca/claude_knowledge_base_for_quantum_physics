## Summary

CERN (Conseil Européen pour la Recherche Nucléaire, or European Organization for Nuclear Research) is the world's largest particle physics laboratory, located near Geneva on the Swiss-French border. Founded in 1954, CERN operates the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and hosts the world's only antimatter factory, capable of producing 20 million antiprotons per minute. CERN is the global hub for antimatter research, hosting experiments (ALPHA-g, BASE, GBAR) that study antihydrogen properties and test fundamental symmetries of physics.

## Resumo

CERN (Conseil Européen pour la Recherche Nucléaire, ou Organização Europeia para Pesquisa Nuclear) é o laboratório de física de partículas mais grande do mundo, localizado perto de Genebra, na fronteira franco-suíça. Fundado em 1954, CERN opera o Grande Colisor de Hádrons (LHC) e hospeda a única fábrica de antimatéria do mundo, capaz de produzir 20 milhões de antiprotons por minuto. CERN é o centro global de pesquisa em antimatéria, hospedando experimentos (ALPHA-g, BASE, GBAR) que estudam propriedades de anti-hidrogênio e testam simetrias fundamentais da física.

## Details

### History and Mission

- **Founded:** 1954, by 12 founding member states
- **Original Purpose:** Peaceful nuclear physics research in post-WWII Europe
- **Membership:** 23 member states (as of 2026), with additional associate and observer members
- **Mission:** Fundamental physics research; advancing scientific knowledge and technology

### Facilities

**Large Hadron Collider (LHC)**
- 27-kilometer underground ring straddling Swiss-French border
- Protons accelerated to 99.9999999% light speed
- Generates ~600 million collisions per second
- Discovered Higgs boson (2012)
- Continues searches for dark matter, extra dimensions, and beyond-Standard-Model physics

**Proton Synchrotron (PS)**
- Older, smaller ring (circumference: 628 meters)
- Used as injector for LHC
- Also feeds the antimatter factory with protons at 99.93% light speed

**Antiproton Factory**
- Downstream from PS
- **Antimatter Production Target:** Iridium rod, 3 mm diameter, 55 mm long
- **Output:** ~20 million antiprotons per minute (40 million every 2 minutes)
- **Antimatter Cost:** Estimated $100 trillion per gram
- **ELENA Ring:** Extra Low Energy Antiproton ring (installed 2015–2016) slows antiprotons to 1.5% light speed

**Experimental Halls**
- Multiple detector caverns housing experiments (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, LHCb)
- Dedicated antimatter facility hosting ALPHA-g, BASE, GBAR experiments

### Antimatter Research at CERN

**Key Experiments**

| Experiment | Purpose | Key Results |
|---|---|---|
| **ALPHA-g** | Measure gravitational acceleration of antihydrogen | Antihydrogen falls down (~75% ± large errors) |
| **BASE** | Measure antiproton magnetic moment | Consistent with proton moment (CPT symmetry test) |
| **GBAR** | Ultra-precise gravity measurement using cold antihydrogen ions | In development; aims for 1% precision |

**Research Questions**
- Does antimatter fall like matter? (ALPHA-g answers: yes, with large error bars)
- Is CPT symmetry exact? (BASE answers: yes, to 1 part in 10⁹)
- Can we cool antihydrogen enough for 1% gravity precision? (GBAR pursuing this)
- Does matter-antimatter asymmetry have a signature in antimatter properties? (Underlying motivation)

### Technology and Innovation

- **Penning Trap Development:** Superconducting magnets + electric fields enable 600+ day antiproton storage
- **Portable Antimatter Traps:** BASE developed movable Penning trap; March 2026 transport test successful
- **Particle Acceleration:** Pioneered techniques now used worldwide
- **Computing:** Handles petabytes of data; drives advances in distributed computing and machine learning

## Contributions to the Field

- **Higgs Boson Discovery** (2012) — Experimental confirmation of the Higgs particle
- **CP Violation Studies** — Precision measurements of matter-antimatter asymmetries
- **Antimatter Confinement** — Developed Penning trap technology for long-term storage
- **Fundamental Symmetry Tests** — Ongoing precision tests of CPT, Lorentz invariance, quantum mechanics
- **Quark-Gluon Plasma** — Created and studied conditions of early universe

### Outreach and Collaboration

- **Open Science:** Raw LHC data publicly available; enables independent research
- **Education:** Hosts student programs, lectures, and public visitors
- **International Collaboration:** Thousands of physicists from 100+ countries work at CERN
- **Technology Transfer:** CERN-developed technologies have commercial applications (medical imaging, superconductors)
