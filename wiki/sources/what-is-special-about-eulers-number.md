## Summary

This 3Blue1Brown video explains why Euler's number *e* ≈ 2.71828 is the most natural base for exponential functions in calculus. The key insight: *e* is the unique base where the derivative of a^t is proportional to itself with proportionality constant 1 (i.e., d/dt(e^t) = e^t). This special property emerges from analyzing growth phenomena like population doubling, where the rate of change is proportional to the current state.

## Resumo

Este vídeo do 3Blue1Brown explica por que o número de Euler *e* ≈ 2,71828 é a base mais natural para funções exponenciais no cálculo. O insight principal: *e* é a base única em que a derivada de a^t é proporcional a si mesma com constante de proporcionalidade 1 (ou seja, d/dt(e^t) = e^t). Essa propriedade especial surge ao analisar fenômenos de crescimento, onde a taxa de mudança é proporcional ao estado atual.

## Key Points

- **Exponentials and derivatives:** For any base *a*, the derivative of a^t equals a^t times a proportionality constant
- **The proportionality constant:** For base 2, it's ln(2) ≈ 0.6931; for base 3, it's ln(3) ≈ 1.0986
- **Euler's number is special:** *e* is defined as the unique base where the proportionality constant equals 1
- **Natural logarithm connection:** The proportionality constant for base *a* is always ln(*a*), answering the "mystery constants"
- **Applications in nature:** Phenomena with rates proportional to current state (population growth, cooling, compound interest) naturally fit the form e^(ct)
